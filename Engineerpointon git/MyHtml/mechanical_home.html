    Tolerance

    Tolerance itself means scope of deviation in dimension. It sepecifies what can be the maximun and minimum deviation for a given dimension. Tolerance has a upper deviation limit and lower deviation limit. With that we can specify a upper dimension i.e what can be the maximum dimension and lower dimension i.e. what can be the minimum dimension. For example. 25Â±0.2mm. Here we have upper deviation of 0.2mm and lower deviation of 0.2mm. So we can now calculate 25+0.2=25.2mm is the upperlimit of the dimesion gievn. Now 25-0.2=24.8mm is the lower limit. Now we can calculate Tolerance. It is Upper limit - Lower limit. i.e. 25.2 - 24.8=0.4mm is the tolerance. There are standard tables specified by ISO standard for tolerance classes. You can find in the table below deviations for a given dimension. Now that you have understood the tolerance calculation, with the help of calculator given below find out the tolerance you want.

    Tolerance Standard

    In ISO standard we have DIN ISO 286-2 (1990-11), here some tolerance class defined with every dimension. eg 20 H6, here 20 is nominal dimension and H6 is tolerance class.     Fits

    Fits define the connection between assembled parts. There are three different types of fits .i.e clearance, transition and interference. Clearance fit means the movement between the consecutive surface will be free. And with no friction. Next is transition fit defines that the movement will be press fit type. That is it will allow some friction. Next is insterference fit, it says that there will be no movement between surfaces. It specifies that once the asssembly is done it will be very rigid. Fits are usually between holes and shaft. To calculate fits we must know the tolerance calculation. There are several tolerance classes. We denote them by capital letters (for holes) and small letters(for shafts). For example30H8/f7.

    Here maximum clearance and minimum clearance both are positive hence its a clearance fit.

  • If maximum clearance is positive and minimum clearance is negative then the fit is transition.
  • If maximum clearance and minimum clearance are negative then the fit is interference.

    Turning

    Turning is the process in which workpiece is clamped in a rotating chuck and the cutting tools are clamped and moved against it.

    Turning Process

  • Drilling

    Drill is clamped in a chuck, installed in tail stock, and the workpiece is clamped in chuck installed in headstock. The drill is fixed and workpiece rotates. The drill chuck is brought near and the chuck is feeded eith the handle available at the tailstock.

  • Facing Turning

    The cutter is clamped in a certain angle and feeded against the flat surface of the workpiece. This process is called Facing.

  • Plain Turning

    The cutter is clamped at right angle against the rotating axis of the workpiece. The cutter is given the feed depth and moved in paralled to axis. This is plain turning

    • Milling

    The process of machining flat, curved or irregular surface by feeding the workpiece against a rotating cutter containing a number of cutting edges.

    • Up Milling

  • Cutter rotates against the direction of feed
  • Also known as conventional milling
  • Chip width is minimum at initial stage and increases with feed. It is maximum at the end of feed.
  • Heat is diffuse to the work piece which causes change in material properties
  • Tool wear is more because the tool runs against the feed.
  • Tool life is short.
  • Less surface finish
  • High quality cutting fluid required
  • High cutting force needed

    • Down Milling

  • Cutter rotates with the direction of feed
  • Also known as climb milling
  • Chip size is maximum at start of the cut And decreases with the feed. It's minimum at the end of feed.
  • No channge in material properties
  • Wear is less as compared as compare to up milling as to cutter rotates with the feed.
  • Tool life is high
  • More finishing than up milling
  • Normal quality cutting fluid will do.
  • Low cutting force is required.
